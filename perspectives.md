# Idea Spark — Innovation Perspective Knowledge Base

7 meta-categories with sub-perspectives for systematically exploring research innovation space in organizational behavior and management.

---

## 1. 翻转维度 / Dimension Flip

把已有研究的某个维度反过来。最常用的创新路径之一。

### 1a. 长期→短期 (Long-term → Short-term)

- **操作**: 关注短期影响，可能与长期影响不同甚至相反
- **典型情境**: ESM（经验取样法）研究特别适合此视角，因为可以捕捉即时效应
- **范文**: Qin, Huang, Johnson, Hu, & Ju (2018). The Short-lived Benefits of Abusive Supervisory Behavior for Actors. *AMJ*, 61, 1951-1975.
  - 核心创新：辱虐管理对施暴者有短期收益（恢复感、工作投入），挑战了"辱虐只对受害者有害"的共识
- **范文**: Tepper et al. (2018). Examining Follower Responses to Transformational Leadership from a Dynamic, Person-Environment Fit Perspective. *AMJ*, 61, 1343-1368.
  - 核心创新：变革型领导的效果是动态波动的，不是静态的

### 1b. 对人→对己 (Other → Self)

- **操作**: 关注行为对自己的影响（而非对他人的影响）
- **典型情境**: 领导行为如何影响领导者自身？帮助他人如何影响帮助者？
- **范文**: Lin, Scott, & Matta (2019). The Dark Side of Transformational Leader Behaviors for Leaders Themselves. *AMJ*, 62, 1556-1582.
  - 核心创新：变革型领导消耗领导者自身资源，带来负面效应
- **范文**: Qin, Huang, Johnson, Hu, & Ju (2018). 同1a范文，也是"对己"视角的范例

### 1c. 对己→对人 (Self → Other / Interpersonal Transmission)

- **操作**: 关注个人特质/行为的人际传递性影响
- **典型情境**: 员工特质影响配偶、同事、上司的行为
- **范文**: 倪丹, 刘琛琳, 郑晓明 (2021). 员工正念对配偶家庭满意度和工作投入的影响. *心理学报*, 53(02), 199-214.
  - 核心创新：员工的正念不仅影响自己，还通过家庭机制影响配偶的工作状态

### 1d. 自上→自下 (Top-down → Bottom-up)

- **操作**: 上下级方向翻转，下属影响上级
- **典型情境**: 下属行为如何塑造领导风格？下属如何通过日常互动改变领导？
- **范文**: Desai & Kouchaki (2017). Moral Symbols: A Necklace of Garlic against Unethical Requests. *AMJ*, 60, 7-28.
  - 核心创新：下属的道德符号（如佩戴道德相关饰品）能自下而上抑制领导的不道德要求
- **范文**: Kim & Kim (2020). Does Negative Feedback Benefit (or Harm) Recipient Creativity? *AMJ*, 63, 584-612.
  - 核心创新：负反馈的效果取决于反馈的方向（上级→下级 vs 下级→上级）

### 1e. 结果→前因 (Outcome → Antecedent)

- **操作**: 把传统的因变量（结果）变成自变量（前因）
- **典型情境**: OCB、Voice、Creativity、绩效等作为前因如何？
- **范文**: Koopman, Lanaj, & Scott (2016). Integrating the Bright and Dark Sides of OCB. *AMJ*, 59, 414-435.
  - 核心创新：帮助他人（OCB）作为前因，既有收益（声誉）也有代价（资源消耗）
- **范文**: Khan, Moss, Quratulain, & Hameed (2018). When and How Subordinate Performance Leads to Abusive Supervision. *JOM*, 44, 2801-2826.
  - 核心创新：下属绩效（通常作为结果变量）反过来引发辱虐管理
- **范文**: McClean, Martin, Emich, & Woodruff (2018). The Social Consequences of Voice. *AMJ*, 61, 1869-1891.
  - 核心创新：建言行为（voice）作为前因，影响建言者在团队中的地位

### 1f. bright↔dark (Valence Flip)

- **操作**: 正面/负面翻转——好事的坏的一面，坏事的好的一面
- **典型情境**: 任何被公认为"好"或"坏"的构念
- **范文**: Shin & Grant (2019). Bored by Interest: How Intrinsic Motivation in One Task Can Reduce Performance on Other Tasks. *AMJ*, 62, 415-436.
  - 核心创新：内在动机（公认的"好事"）可能损害其他任务的绩效
- **范文**: Lee & Duffy (2019). A Functional Model of Workplace Envy. *AMJ*, 62, 1085-1110.
  - 核心创新：嫉妒（公认的"坏事"）可以促进从嫉妒对象那里学习
- **范文**: Humphrey, Ashforth, & Diefendorff (2015). The Bright Side of Emotional Labor. *JOB*, 36, 749-769.
  - 核心创新：情绪劳动可以是积极的

## 2. 替换构念 / Construct Swap

换一个新变量或反面变量。

### 2a. 新理论/新变量 (New Construct)

- **操作**: 引入一个新构念到OB领域，或设计新的变量
- **典型情境**: 从其他学科引入概念、或发现新的现象可以量化
- **范文**: Ozcelik & Barsade (2018). No Employee an Island: Workplace Loneliness and Job Performance. *AMJ*, 61, 2343-2366.
  - 核心创新：把"孤独感"这个普遍现象正式引入OB领域作为研究变量
- **注意**: 此视角创新性最高，但风险也最大（新变量需要大量验证工作）

### 2b. 反面变量 (Construct Opposite)

- **操作**: 取一个已有变量的"反面"——领导力→追随力，voice→silence，humility→arrogance
- **典型情境**: 当某个构念已经被充分研究，其对立面可能未被探索
- **示例**: 领导力→追随力(followership)，voice→silence，self-efficacy→self-doubt，justice→injustice

## 3. 切换层次 / Level Shift

改变分析水平或领域。

### 3a. 个体间→个体内 (Between-person → Within-person)

- **操作**: 从"人与人之间的差异"转向"同一个人在不同时间的差异"
- **典型情境**: ESM/日记研究，关注个体内的波动和变化
- **注意**: 这是方法创新（ESM）和视角创新（短期vs长期）的结合

### 3b. 工作→生活 (Work → Home / Domain Change)

- **操作**: 工作场所的行为/特质如何溢出到家庭/生活领域
- **典型情境**: 工作-家庭界面研究
- **范文**: Harrison & Wagner (2016). Spilling Outside the Box: The Effects of Individuals' Creative Behaviors at Work on Time Spent with Their Spouses at Home. *AMJ*, 59, 841-859.
  - 核心创新：工作中的创造力行为如何影响在家与配偶相处的时间
- **范文**: Lin, Ilies, Pluut, & Pan (2017). You are a helpful co-worker, but do you support your spouse? *OBHDP*, 138, 45-58.
  - 核心创新：工作中的帮助行为可能消耗资源，导致在家中无法支持配偶

### 3c. 任务→任务 (Task → Task Cross-contamination)

- **操作**: 一个任务上的行为/状态如何影响另一个任务
- **范文**: Shin & Grant (2019). 同1f范文——一个任务上的内在动机降低另一个任务的绩效

## 4. 重新组合 / Recombination

整合不同文献产生独特贡献。

### 4a. 文献整合 (Literature Integration)

- **操作**: 找到两个通常不对话的文献领域，将它们的理论整合
- **典型情境**: 两个领域各自有成熟理论，但没有人把它们联系起来
- **范文**: Wu, Parker, Wu, & Lee (2018). When and Why People Engage in Different Forms of Proactive Behavior. *AMJ*, 61, 293-323.
  - 核心创新：非常出色地整合了 self-construal 文献和 proactivity 文献，产生独特贡献
- **关键**: 整合不能是"两个文献各说一半"，而是要解释 WHY 它们在一起能产生新洞察

### 4b. Congruence / 一致性视角

- **操作**: 关注两个变量之间的匹配/不一致，而非单个变量的影响
- **典型情境**: 上下级之间的价值观/性格/期望是否一致
- **范文**: Baer, Frank, Matta, Luciano, & Wellman (2021). Undertrusted, Overtrusted, or Just Right? *AMJ*, 64, 180-206.
  - 核心创新：信任的"过多"与"过少"都不好，关注信任的匹配程度
- **范文**: Zhang & Wang (2012). Leader-follower congruence in proactive personality. *AMJ*, 55, 111-130.
  - 核心创新：领导-下属的前瞻性人格一致性影响LMX和工作结果

## 5. 刷新情境 / Context Refresh

同一问题换个场景。

### 5a. 新情境/新文化 (New Context)

- **操作**: 把经典研究放到新的文化或特殊情境中，发现原有结论可能不成立甚至相反
- **典型情境**: 中国情境、特殊群体（难民、创业者等）、特殊时期（COVID等）
- **范文**: Qin et al. (2022). Adults still can't resist: A social robot can induce normative conformity. *Computers in Human Behavior*, 127, 107041.
  - 核心创新：把经典的从众实验放到人机互动情境中
- **注意**: 仅仅"换一个文化背景"已不够创新，需要说明为什么新情境会导致不同的结论

### 5b. 热点嫁接 (Hot Topic Grafting)

- **操作**: 将经典理论与当下社会热点结合
- **典型情境**: AI与工作、远程办公、ESG/可持续发展、平台经济
- **注意**: 热点嫁接的关键不是"蹭热点"，而是解释经典理论在新情境下的新机制
- **2024-2026热点参考**: AI替代焦虑、人机协作、混合办公、心理健康、DEI（多元平等包容）、ESG

## 6. 刷新方法 / Method Refresh

用新方法研究老问题。

### 6a. 方法论创新 (Methodological Innovation)

- **操作**: 用新的研究方法研究已有研究问题
- **典型情境**: 当某个研究问题主要依赖横截面问卷时，用ESM或实验可以提供更强的因果推断
- **方法选项**: ESM/日记研究、field experiment、经验取样法、社会网络分析、元分析、二手数据分析、多源多层、Panel data

### 6b. 数据/分析反常规 (Unconventional Data/Analysis)

- **操作**: 用跟大多数人不一样的方式搜集数据或分析数据
- **典型情境**: 用方差/标准差创造新变量（如LMX Differentiation），用多层模型替代单层
- **注意**: 方法创新本身不是目的，要服务于研究问题的深入

## 7. 从好奇出发 / Curiosity-Driven

不填空，问"这有趣吗"。最重要的创新来源之一。

### 7a. 挑战共识 (Challenge Consensus)

- **操作**: 质疑领域的大小假设和"常识"
- **典型情境**: 最好的研究从此而来。机会比想象的多，self-efficacy可能是最大的天花板
- **范文**: Dai, Dietvorst, Tuckfield, Milkman, & Schweitzer (2018). Quitting When the Going Gets Tough: A Downside of High Performance Expectations. *AMJ*, 61, 1667-1691.
  - 核心创新：高绩效期望（公认的好事）反而让人在困难面前更容易放弃
- **范文**: Qin, Chen, Yam, Huang, & Ju (2020). The Double-Edged Sword of Leader Humility. *JAP*, 105, 693-712.
  - 核心创新：领导谦逊（公认的好品质）在某些条件下反而促进下属偏差行为

### 7b. 有趣的问题 (Interesting Questions)

- **操作**: 从"这很有趣"出发，而非"这填补了空白"
- **典型情境**: 观察到一个有趣的现象、一个反直觉的发现、一个令人好奇的问题
- **范文**: Ozcelik & Barsade (2018). No Employee an Island: Workplace Loneliness and Job Performance. *AMJ*, 61, 2343-2366.
  - 核心创新：把一个普遍但未被研究的现象（职场孤独感）正式引入学术研究
- **范文**: To, Kilduff, Ordonez, & Schweitzer (2018). Going for It on Fourth Down: Rivalry Increases Risk Taking. *AMJ*, 61, 1281-1306.
  - 核心创新：竞争关系如何影响风险承担——从橄榄球的四档强攻行为出发
- **关键**: "有趣"不等于"随意"。有趣的问题背后通常有深刻的逻辑

### 7c. 老问题新思路 (Classic Problem, New Thinking)

- **操作**: 对一个经典的研究问题提供全新的思考角度
- **典型情境**: 某个研究问题已经被研究了几十年，但一直缺乏令人满意的答案
- **范文**: Kim & Kim (2020). 同1d范文——负反馈的效果取决于方向性，这提供了一个全新的思考角度

## Depth Criteria — What Makes Each Category Deep vs Shallow

Use these criteria to evaluate whether an idea generated from each category is genuinely deep or merely a surface-level variation.

### 1. 翻转维度 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The flip reveals a NEW causal mechanism — it explains WHY the reverse direction operates through different theoretical logic. The flipped relationship is surprising because existing theory predicts the opposite.

**Shallow**: The flip only changes direction/valence without new explanatory logic. "X affects Y" → "Y affects X" without explaining why the reverse mechanism differs.

**Test**: Can you explain WHY the flipped relationship works differently without saying "because it's the opposite"?

### 2. 替换构念 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The new construct captures a phenomenon that existing constructs miss — it names something real that people experience but scholars haven't formalized. It opens new theoretical terrain.

**Shallow**: The new construct is just a synonym or narrow specification of an existing one without explaining what's uniquely different.

**Test**: Would a reasonable person say "yes, that's a genuinely different thing"?

### 3. 切换层次 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The level shift reveals that the SAME phenomenon operates through DIFFERENT mechanisms at different levels.

**Shallow**: The level shift only changes the method (ESM instead of survey) without discovering that the mechanism differs across levels.

**Test**: Is the theoretical story different at the new level, or is it the same story with more data points?

### 4. 重新组合 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The two literatures genuinely illuminate each other — combining them produces insights that neither could generate alone.

**Shallow**: The two literatures are placed side by side without true integration.

**Test**: Can you state a specific insight that requires BOTH literatures to generate?

### 5. 刷新情境 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The new context reveals that existing theory's predictions are WRONG or incomplete in this setting.

**Shallow**: The new context produces the same results as the original, just in a different setting.

**Test**: Does the new context break or refine the original theory, or just add another data point?

### 6. 刷新方法 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The new method reveals something that the old method systematically missed — not just more precision, but a different picture.

**Shallow**: The new method only adds rigor to the same theoretical question.

**Test**: Does the new method change what you can SEE, or just how precisely you see it?

### 7. 从好奇出发 — Deep vs Shallow

**Deep**: The question challenges a core assumption that the field takes for granted. If true, it would require rethinking a body of work.

**Shallow**: The question is interesting but challenges a peripheral assumption.

**Test**: Would people say "that changes how I think about X" or "that's a clever study"?

## Usage Notes for the LLM

1. When diagnosing, assess all 7 meta-categories first. Only expand sub-perspectives for 🔓 categories.
2. Each sub-perspective's anchor paper is a reference point, NOT a template to copy. The goal is to identify what makes the anchor paper innovative and apply the same thinking pattern differently.
3. When multiple sub-perspectives apply, prioritize by: novelty potential > theoretical depth > feasibility.
4. The "从好奇出发" category should always be considered, even if the direction seems well-explored.
5. Perspectives are starting points for thinking, not formulas to fill in. The best research combines multiple perspectives in creative ways.
6. **Use the Depth Criteria above** when generating ideas. Aim for "Deep" ideas. If an idea only meets the "Shallow" standard, either refine it or don't include it — 3 deep ideas beat 6 shallow ones.
